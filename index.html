Frequently Asked Questions

Should I open a UK bank account?
It is very useful to open a bank account once you arrive in Edinburgh. You may need to pay for accommodation, tuition fees or household bills by direct debit. You may also receive money directly into your account, from job wages, grant/bursary/loan (if applicable). Learn more at the university's student banking site.
What kind of computer should I get?
This is completely up to personal preference. If you have concerns over work, that are 10 years old are still able to run programming languages completely fine. The only other considerations are personal ones, such as how much money you’re willing to spend, whether you like to play games, etc.
Do postgraduates have a Fresher's Week?
Fresher's week is part of Welcome Week, which has plenty of events open to everyone, as well as some specifically targeted towards postgraduates.
Are there restaurants and cafes on campus?
There are plenty of places to eat on campus, including many cafes that also serve coffee and tea. Cafes are located in the Main Library, Appleton Tower, 29 Buccleuch Place, and David Hume Tower. In addition to cafes, food stands serving food and coffee are located outside throughout George Square. If you desire more variety, there are plenty of other restaurants and shops located within a 5 minute walk of campus.
Where can I buy school supplies and university branded clothing?
University clothing can be purchased at the visitor center, as well as at the online gift shop. School supplied can be purchased at a variety of shops nearby the university. To learn more about shopping nearby, visit the university's shopping webpage.
How do I get my student card?
If you've already submitted your photo for your card, you can collect it from the Main Library Helpdesk, George Square when you arrive in Edinburgh. If you haven't yet submitted a photograph, you should do so immediately. Follow the instructions here.
Are there exercise facilities on campus?
The Centre for Sport and Exercise (CSE) offers superb facilities and caters for a diverse range of users. It includes the Pleasance sports centre, FASIC physiotherapy, Peffermill playing fields and Firbush outdoor centre. For more information, visit their website.
Can I join an informatics research team?
Research teams within the School of Informatics are very competitive and are commonly made up of postgraduate students to be involved. If you are an undergraduate, it is advised that you wait until you have more experience.
Other questions...
If you have further questions regarding student life and welcome week, feel free to reach out to any of EUSA's welcome week volunteers. You can also inquire at the Students' Association reception, open daily in Teviot and Potterrow from 9am – 5pm.

Your First Week in the College of Informatics

Personal Tutor

Every student has a Personal Tutor, a member of informatics teaching staff who provides academic support. You will be automatically allocated a personal tutor and are required to have a one on one meeting during your first week here. During this first meeting, they will help you:

  • Advise you on course choices
  • Register you for courses
To contact your Personal Tutor, go to MyEd. Then in the navigation bar, select "Studies" to view a sub menu. Finally select "Personal Tutor" to view the name and contact information of your tutor. Contact them to request your first week meeting.

Preparing for Courses

Timetable

Most Informatics courses will consist of lectures and tutorials. For detailed information on the location and time of your lectures and tutorials, check your timetable. Once you register for courses, an automatically created timetable will be available for you on MyEd. To view your timetable, select "Studies" in the naviagation bar to view a sub menu and then select "Timetabling."

Tutorials

Tutorials occur on average once a week and are most often conducted in Forrest Hill. Depending on your course, you will either be automatically assigned a tutorial section or your course organizer will create an alternative way to choose your section. You may not this information until your first lecture of the course.

Dice

What Is Dice

DICE stands for Distributed Informatics Computing Environment. It is the computing service developed, maintained and supported by University of Edinburgh. There are DICE computers (Linux desktop PCs) in the public spaces and meeting rooms of the Informatics Forum and Forrest Hill.

Setting Up a Dice Account

You need DICE Account before you can access any of the computing services provided by Informatics. This account allows you to log in to any of the DICE computers. A DICE account will be created automatically for you if you are a new informatics student and an email will be sent to you that allows you to create a password. Alternatively, you can set up the password on the Informatics Password Portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Informatics Forum?
The Forum is the main Informatics research building. It houses professors and post-graduate researchers. Other students do not have general access to this building and must have an appointment or meeting inside to use it.
Where are lectures given if not in the Forum?
Lectures are commonly given in lecture halls around the main campus. Appleton Tower is used often for informatics lectures.
Are there labs/study space/library facilities in the Forum?
These resources in the Forum are only for research related work and study. They are not available for masters and undergraduate students.
Where are the labs?
Labs are conducted during tutorial sections. These are most often held in Forrest Hill, but check with your course organizer for the specific location and time of your lab.
What is Forrest Hill?
Forrest Hill is the current location of the Informatics Graduate School and Teaching Organisation. It houses the Support Office for any informatics students, tutorials, and study spaces. It also has Dice computers, printers, and a microwave. It is located just off Forrest Road.
