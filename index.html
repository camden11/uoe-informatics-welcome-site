Getting to Campus

The George Square campus is located within walking distance of all student accomodation. The campus is also accessable via a few different forms of public transportation, including university-run shuttles and the city's Lothian busses. For more information, vist the University's transport webpage.

Welcome Week Events

During Welcome Week, the University holds a wide variety of events designed to prepare you for your semester. Whether it's meeting other students, learning more about your studies, or finding out how to get involved, you're sure to find something that interests you during your first week in Edinburgh.

Academic and Extracurricular Events

During your time at The University of Edinburgh, you'll be faced with a lot of decisions about what classes to take and what groups to join. At the academic, societies, and sports fairs, you'll gain a lot of valuable information to help you decide what paths to take during the semester.

Social Events

Throughout welcome week, the Edinburgh University Student Association (EUSA) runs social events ranging from club nights to cultural discussions to tours of the city. With hundreds of events going on through the week, you're sure to find something you enjoy. For a full list of Welcome Week social events, take a look at the Welcome Week What's On Guide.

Your First Week in the College of Informatics

Personal Tutor

Every student has a Personal Tutor, a member of informatics teaching staff who provides academic support. You will be automatically allocated a personal tutor and are required to have a one on one meeting during your first week here. During this first meeting, they will help you:

  • Advise you on course choices
  • Register you for courses
To contact your Personal Tutor, go to MyEd. Then in the navigation bar, select "Studies" to view a sub menu. Finally select "Personal Tutor" to view the name and contact information of your tutor. Contact them to request your first week meeting.

Informatics Courses

Timetable

Most Informatics courses will consist of lectures and tutorials. For detailed information on the location and time of your lectures and tutorials, check your timetable. Once you register for courses, an automatically created timetable will be available for you on MyEd. To view your timetable, select "Studies" in the naviagation bar to view a sub menu and then select "Timetabling."

Lectures

Lectures are taught on the main campus.

Tutorials

Tutorials occur on average once a week and are most often conducted in Forrest Hill. Depending on your course, you will either be automatically assigned a tutorial section or your course organizer will create an alternative way to choose your section. You may not this information until your first lecture of the course.

Dice

What Is Dice

DICE stands for Distributed Informatics Computing Environment. It is the computing service developed, maintained and supported by University of Edinburgh. There are DICE computers (Linux desktop PCs) in the public spaces and meeting rooms of the Informatics Forum and Forrest Hill.

Setting Up a Dice Account

You need DICE Account before you can access any of the computing services provided by Informatics. This account allows you to log in to any of the DICE computers. A DICE account will be created automatically for you if you are a new informatics student and an email will be sent to you that allows you to create a password. Alternatively, you can set up the password on the Informatics Password Portal.
